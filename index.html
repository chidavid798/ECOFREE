NO DIRT IS TOO DIRTY FOR US

Ecofree Waste Industries, Inc. is a new company based in Warri,Delta State Nigeria. We are a vertically integrated company that is owned and operated by GroupRed&Co solid waste collection businesses, recycling facilities, and landfills. It is a leader in the solid waste industry in Nigeria. It serves more than 10 million residential, commercial and industrial customers across 128 major markets in 36 states. After purchasing Warri-based giant waste hauler Ejiro&Family Industries (EFI) in 2021, together with private equity firms The Mama Egime Group and Apollo Management. Ecofree Waste Industries, Inc. became the second largest non-hazardous solid waste management company in Nigeria.

The company had a plan to reduce its local carbon footprint by more than 3.3 million pounds each year in Warri, Delta State, by converting its monthly 80,000 gallons of petroleum diesel to B20 bio-diesel fuel. Ecofree Waste converted the local 225-truck fleet to the clean-burning B20. Ecofree Waste was a member of the Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design (LEEDS) Program which is outlined by the U.S. Green Building Council. Onyeka GroupRed, chairman and chief executive officer of Ecofree Waste, said, "Ecofree Waste has long been an active steward of our environment...we aim to employ environmentally responsible and sustainable practices." Ecofree Waste also implemented innovations such as gas-to-energy projects. Gas-to-energy converts waste gas from landfills into electricity which offsets conventional use of oil and natural gas

TYPES OF WASTES

  1. Plastic Wastes

    Plastic pollution has become one of the most pressing environmental issues, as rapidly increasing production of disposable plastic products overwhelms the world’s ability to deal with them. Plastic pollution is most visible in developing Asian and African nations, where garbage collection systems are often inefficient or nonexistent. But the developed world, especially in countries with low recycling rates, also has trouble properly collecting discarded plastics. Plastic trash has become so ubiquitous it has prompted efforts to write a global treaty negotiated by the United Nations.

  2. Organic Wastes

    Organic waste, or biodegradable waste, is a natural refuse type that comes from plants or animals. It comes in manifold forms – biodegradable plastics, food waste, green waste, paper waste, manure, human waste, sewage, and slaughterhouse waste.
